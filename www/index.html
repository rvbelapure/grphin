About this Graph:

How are employees moving between companies?

We seek to answer this question by building a directed graph, in which:
  • Nodes represent companies
  • Edges represent the flow of people between two companies
About the Data:

We parsed 7,207,357 LinkedIn public profiles - which resulted in a graph containing 3,215,402 nodes and 3,574,637 unique edges.

Next we filter out “uninteresting” nodes and edges by setting a threshold. In this case, nodes and edges that appeared in <40 profiles are discarded (with a few whitelisted exemptions). The result is a disconnected graph with isolated groups of nodes and edges.

Next, we build a single connected graph, by running breadth-first-search from the single largest company node (IBM) in the original graph.

The resulting graph has 51 companies, along with 121 unique edges.
